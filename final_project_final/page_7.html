7. To Gay or Not too Gay

AJ Brown is our hero of this episode - the first and only gay hero of Queer Eye! He was nominated by his best friend Al for "needing work in a lot of different areas." Particularly, he acts like different people at work, with his friends and boyfriend, and with his family. Not to mention, he is not openly gay and is very self-conscious of "looking gay." His biggest life regret is not coming out to his dad before he died. With things getting serious with his boyfriend, he wants help coming out to his stepmom. Throughout the episode, AJ grapples with not wanted to appear too "feminine" or being afraid people will be able to tell his sexual preference just by looking at him. The Fab 5 eventually teach him that being your true self isn't going to offend anybody. when he does come out to his stepmom at the end, its one of the most emotional scenes in Queer Eye history. He reads her a letter he meant to read to his late father, crying and apologizing for hiding this part of him for so long. His stepmom is loving and supportive in return, and for that it was the happiest of endings.
